Osteoarthritis

Osteoarthritis (OA) is the most frequent kind of arthritis, affecting more than four million Canadians. This is characterized by the degradation of cartilage, a tough, elastic material, which covers and protects the ends of bones.
Cartilage is an essential component of the joints. It acts as a shock absorber, and also enables the joint to move smoothly.

With Osteoarthritis, the cartilage erodes, eventually resulting in pain,stiffness, swelling and bone-on-bone movement in the affected joint.

Are you suffering from osteoarthritis?

DO YOU HAVE KNEE, HIP OR SHOULDER PAIN?

You may be experiencing pain due to inflammation of the lining of the joint. . Symptoms such as redness, or hot swollen joints are characteristics of osteoarthritis. This pain prevents normal use of the joint resulting in reduced of function of the joint and weak muscles around the joints.

Please take our self-assessment test to determine if you have Osteoarthritis symptoms that need to be dealt with. Like many ailments, the sooner you seek treatment, the better the chances of causing less damage to the joint. Please print and share the self-assessment results with your doctor.
