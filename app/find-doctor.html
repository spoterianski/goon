Tips for selecting your Osteoarthritis specialist

A successful relationship between patient and doctor is one of the key factors in the management of Osteoarthritis. You must trust your doctor and feel comfortable with him/her to warrantee the success of your treatment. Your specialist should be recognized as clinical but should pay attention to the need of the patient taking time for them To find a Specialist you can start asking friends and family, ask for recommendations from your Family Doctor, search the internet about specialists in Osteoarthritis near your location or you can use our FIND A DOCTOR system to find one option near your area.

To select a specialist:

FAMILY DOCTORS: Their responsibility is usually to treat non-urgent topics related with health. For example, building and maintaining your personal profile related with health, yearly check-ups and non-emergency illnesses. Your Family Doctor may refer you to a specialist in Osteoarthritis (like Orthopedist, Rheumatologist or Sport Medicine). You can suggest to your family doctor the name of one specialist to be referred, for this reason your research is important. ORTHOPAEDIST: These specialists are doctors with studies, training and experience managing problems like injuries in bones, joints and muscles in the body. The treatments that Orthopaedists may suggest will depend of the severity of your Osteoarthritis; therapies include treatment with pain killers, treatment with prescriptions medications, physiotherapy, injections of steroids or hyaluronic acid or any kind of surgery.

SPORTS MEDICINE SPECIALISTS: These doctors are specialists treating the musculoskeletal system (muscle, ligament, joint, bone) that do not require surgery. Despite the title, Sport Medicine Specialists are able to treat the Osteoarthritis condition for any person, not only athletes. Depending of the severity, Sports Medicine Specialists can suggest treatment with pain killers, treatment with prescriptions medications, physiotherapy, and injections of steroids or hyaluronic acid. If they consider that a surgery is the best option they can refer to an Orthopedic Surgeon to continue with your process.

RHEUMATOLOGISTS: These doctors are trained to treat Rheumatoid Arthritis and/or Osteoarthritis as well as disorders of the musculoskeletal system. Depending of the severity, Rheumatologists can suggest treatment with pain killers, treatment with prescriptions medications, physiotherapy, and injections of steroids or hyaluronic acid. If they consider than a surgery is the best option they can refer to an Orthopedic Surgeon to continue with your process.
About Your Appointment
For chronic conditions like Osteoarthritis, treatment is a long and painful journey; the more information your doctor receives the more effective will be the treatment and management of your condition. Your appointment can be an added value in your Osteoarthritis management.

Some tips for your appointment.

Before your appointment, write and prepare all relevant information that may be useful for better diagnostics:

Exact joint or joints affected

Time when pain or stiffness has started, and how this pain has increased along time

List of all medications and all treatments you are using to manage your pain (including physical therapy or alternative therapy like acupuncture)

List of all other medications you are taking not related with your condition

Information about your allergies

Information about familial conditions

Any activities relating or impacting your condition

List of all activities you are not able to do because of your condition

List of all questions you have related with your condition including your feelings as a consequence of your Osteoarthritis

Prepare all your previous test results (e.g. X-rays, lab tests)

Self-evaluation (available in our OA Management Club)

Bring all the previous information to your next appointment with your doctors and the result will be more effective and you will be part of the successful management of your Osteoarthritis.
